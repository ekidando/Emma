Emmanuel Kidando, Ph.D., P.E.



My research background is interdisciplinary. I have participated in several research projects related to intelligent transportation systems, freeway operations, software systems, and highway safety. I am a passionate civil engineer and data scientist who has broad in-depth knowledge of transportation engineering, programming, and statistical skills. I have used these skills in my research for more than six years.

Also, I have worked on a connected vehicle system evaluation project. As a leading research associate at Florida State University, I developed a methodology for evaluating the connected vehicle system. The main tasks included: testing Dedicated short-range communications technology (DSRC) signal strength using the MCCT, developing algorithms for estimating Automated Traffic Signal Performance Measures (ATSPM), and downloading traffic data in real-time from the BlueToad and crowdsourced systems for operational analysis.
